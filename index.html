Target Species
WHAT YOU CAN CATCH IN ORANGE BEACH

The Gulf of Mexico off Orange Beach, Alabama is one of the most productive saltwater fisheries in the country. From world-class red snapper fishing to trophy yellowfin tuna at the offshore oil rigs, Captain Marty and his crew target a wide variety of species year-round, giving anglers the chance to battle everything from hard-fighting amberjack and redfish to colorful mahi-mahi and acrobatic king mackerel.

🐟

RED SNAPPER

June – July (Federal Season)

The Gulf's most prized fish. Alabama's red snapper fishery is world-class, and Orange Beach is one of the premier destinations. Captain Marty targets them on nearshore and offshore reefs, wrecks, and artificial structures. The federal season is short but incredible — worth planning your entire trip around. Strict regulations but absolutely worth the wait.

💪

AMBERJACK

Year-Round (Closures Vary)

Powerful reef donkeys that test your tackle and your back. Greater amberjack are found on nearshore and offshore structure throughout the Gulf. Average 30-60 lbs with fish exceeding 100 lbs possible. These brutes are one of the hardest-fighting fish in the Gulf — light tackle or conventional, they'll give you the fight of your life.

🔴

RED DRUM (REDFISH)

Year-Round (Peak: Sept – Nov)

Alabama's inshore king. Captain Marty targets bull reds and slot reds in back bays, passes, and grass flats around Orange Beach. Sight-casting in clear water is a specialty. Redfish are one of the most popular gamefish on the Gulf Coast, and the waters around Orange Beach offer some of the best red drum fishing in the state.

🌊

SPECKLED TROUT

Year-Round (Peak: Oct – Apr)

Inshore staple of the Alabama Gulf Coast. Found in bays, passes, and along grass flats around Orange Beach. Speckled trout are excellent eating and a blast on light tackle. Captain Marty targets them year-round, with the best action during the cooler months when trophy-sized specks move into the back bays.

🏋️

COBIA

March – October (Peak: Apr – Jun)

The "lemonfish" is a prized Gulf species that migrates through Orange Beach waters every spring. Captain Marty targets them sight-fishing near buoys, wrecks, and rays. Powerful fighters averaging 30-50 lbs, cobia are one of the most exciting species to target in the Gulf and provide excellent table fare.

⚡

KING MACKEREL

April – November (Peak: Jul – Sep)

Fast, hard-fighting pelagics found nearshore and offshore in the Gulf of Mexico. Trolling with live bait produces explosive strikes that will get your heart racing. King mackerel (kingfish) are great for action-packed trips where you want bent rods and screaming drags all day long.

🐟

YELLOWFIN TUNA

Year-Round (Peak: Jun – Sep)

Deep sea targets found at offshore oil rigs and in blue water far offshore. Yellowfin tuna fight like freight trains and are incredible eating — sashimi-grade right off the boat. These trips run 12+ hours to the rigs but the payoff is some of the most exciting fishing the Gulf of Mexico has to offer.

🌈

MAHI-MAHI (DOLPHIN)

May – September (Peak: Jun – Aug)

Colorful, acrobatic deep sea fish found around weed lines, floating debris, and offshore structure in the Gulf. Mahi-mahi provide fast action and spectacular jumps that make for incredible photos. They're also outstanding table fare — one of the most popular fish on restaurant menus for good reason.

WHY THE GULF OF MEXICO OFF ORANGE BEACH IS WORLD-CLASS

The Gulf of Mexico is one of the most productive bodies of water on Earth, and Orange Beach, Alabama sits at the epicenter of some of the finest fishing the Gulf has to offer. The combination of natural reefs, thousands of artificial reefs and oil rig structures, warm Gulf Stream-influenced currents, and an incredibly diverse marine ecosystem makes this region a year-round fishing paradise.

The Alabama artificial reef program is the largest in the United States, with over 17,000 reef sites deployed off the coast. These reefs attract and hold massive populations of red snapper, amberjack, grouper, triggerfish, and other reef species. Captain Marty has spent 25+ years learning the locations and patterns of these reefs, giving his clients an enormous advantage on the water.

Inshore, the back bays, bayous, and passes of Orange Beach and the surrounding area create prime habitat for redfish, speckled trout, sheepshead, and flounder. The warm, nutrient-rich waters support massive baitfish populations that attract gamefish year-round. Captain Marty catches fresh live bait every morning before each trip, ensuring his clients always have the best possible presentation.

Offshore, the deep blue waters of the Gulf are home to pelagic species like yellowfin tuna, mahi-mahi, wahoo, and even blue marlin. The oil rig platforms create floating ecosystems that concentrate baitfish and attract predators from miles around. Captain Marty's fleet includes boats designed specifically for these long-range offshore expeditions.

When to Fish
ORANGE BEACH FISHING CALENDAR

The Gulf of Mexico offers incredible fishing opportunities year-round. Here's what to expect each season in Orange Beach.

SPRING

March – May

Cobia migration is the highlight of spring fishing in Orange Beach. Bull reds patrol the bays and passes, speckled trout remain strong, and sheepshead feed around bridges and jetties. Nearshore reefs fire up with amberjack and triggerfish as water temperatures rise. Spanish mackerel begin arriving by late April.

CobiaRed DrumSpeckled TroutSheepsheadAmberjackTriggerfishSpanish Mackerel

SUMMER

June – August

Peak season in Orange Beach. The red snapper federal season (June-July) is the main event — book early! King mackerel run strong, yellowfin tuna are at the offshore rigs, and mahi-mahi light up the blue water. Inshore, redfish and speckled trout remain active. This is the busiest time of year.

Red SnapperKing MackerelYellowfin TunaMahi-MahiCobiaRed DrumSpeckled TroutSpanish Mackerel

FALL

September – November

Many consider fall the BEST season for Orange Beach fishing. Bull redfish run hard through the passes, creating epic inshore action. False albacore blitzes light up the nearshore waters. Amberjack reopen on the reefs. Cooler weather means less boat traffic and more fish for you. Excellent inshore and nearshore action across the board.

Red Drum (Bull Reds)False AlbacoreAmberjackSpeckled TroutKing MackerelGrouper

WINTER

December – February

Trophy speckled trout season makes winter fishing in Orange Beach special. Sheepshead stack up around bridges, jetties, and pilings. Fewer crowds mean the water is all yours. Great for dedicated anglers who don't mind cooler weather. Offshore trips when weather permits produce excellent grouper and amberjack.

Trophy Speckled TroutSheepsheadGrouperAmberjackRed Drum

THE ORANGE BEACH FISHING EXPERIENCE

Orange Beach, Alabama is one of the premier fishing destinations on the entire Gulf of Mexico. Located on a barrier island between the Gulf and the Intracoastal Waterway, Orange Beach provides access to an incredible diversity of fishing environments — from protected back bays and bayous to nearshore reefs and deep blue offshore waters. This unique geography means anglers can target dozens of species year-round, from slot redfish in the calm back bays to 200-pound yellowfin tuna at the offshore oil rigs.

The Alabama Gulf Coast benefits from the largest artificial reef program in the country, with over 17,000 deployed reef sites creating habitat that supports massive fish populations. These reefs, combined with natural bottom structure, oil platforms, and the warm currents of the Gulf of Mexico, make the waters off Orange Beach some of the most productive in the world.
